A Legend who reunited whole India

Chanakya (~300 B.C.), also known as Kautilya or Vishnugupt was an Indian teacher, philosopher, economist, jurist and royal advisor. Originally a professor of economics and political science at the ancient Takshashila University (an ancient centre of learning located in north-western ancient India, presently in Pakistan), he managed the first Maurya emperor Chandragupta's rise to power at a young age, and united whole India under the leadership of one king.
He authored the ancient Indian political treatise, the Arthashastra. As such, he is considered as the pioneer of the field of economics and political science in India, and his work is thought of as an important precursor to classical economics.

Biography

  • Chanakya was born in a Brahmin family. He was educated at Takshashila, he later became a teacher (acharya) at the same place. Chanakya's life was connected to two cities: Takshashila and Pataliputra (present-day Patna in Bihar, India). Pataliputra was the capital of the Magadha kingdom. One day Chanakya get to know that 'Alexander the Great' was coming towards India conquering many regions/kingdoms in between. He went to 'Dhana Nanda', king of Magadha, for help, the most strongest kingdom of India that time. But there, Chanakya was insulted by Dhana Nanda and there he took pledge, "until I don't destroy you and put someone worthy on that throne, I will not tie my sacred thread" (not tying sacred thread by Brahmin's was considered a symbol of being on revenge, and withdrawing oneself from other Brahmin's duties).
  • From here he went on to the search of someone worthy of becoming a king who can protect people and fulfill the needs of people before his own. After some days he found a boy named 'Chandragupta', who Chanakya thought is worthy of becoming a true king. He take the boy from his family and trained him in Takshashila. Later when the army of Alexander The Great was approaching near border's of India, Chanakya secretly entered Chandragupta into his army and tried to defeat Alexander internally. It is not confirmed but some sources said, its due to Chanakya's well planned actions of defeating Alexander internally made him sick(poisoned) and later he died due to that, and then their army went back to Macedonia, which eventually saved India from getting conquered.
  • During his life-time he wrote two books: Arthashastra and Neetishastra (also known as 'Chanakya Neeti'). The Arthashastra discusses monetary and fiscal policies, welfare, international relations, and war strategies in detail. The text also outlines the duties of a ruler. Chanakya Neeti is a collection of aphorisms, said to be selected by Chanakya from the various shastras. These books forms the basis of life and war stretegies which are still valid at present. He never married and served his entire life for the betterment of the people of India. His greatness will surely inspire the generations!

Notable works

  • Written books that forms the basis of management, economics, politics, leadership, governance, warfare, military tactics, accounting systems and several others.
  • Decisive internal war against Alexander the Great, saving India from being conquered.
  • Raising Chandragupta to led the foundation of Mauryan Empire.
  • Reuniting entire nation(India) under the leadership of one king, 'Chandragupta Maurya'.
